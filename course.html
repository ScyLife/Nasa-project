Space Launch System

An American two-stage super-heavy launch vehicle developed by NASA for manned missions beyond low Earth orbit. Technologically, SLS relies on the unrealized plans of the Ares-5 launch vehicle under the Constellation program; development is based on the main thrusters and solid boosters of the Space Shuttle program, which ended in 2011

Nil Armstrong

American NASA astronaut, test pilot, space engineer, university professor, US naval pilot, the first person to walk on the moon on July 20, 1969 during the Apollo 11 lunar expedition

Moon

Earth's only natural satellite. The closest satellite of the planet to the Sun, since the planets closest to the Sun do not have them. The second brightest object in the earth's sky after the Sun and the fifth largest natural satellite of a planet in the solar system. The average distance between the centers of the Earth and the Moon is 384467 km.
